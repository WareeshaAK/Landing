NAVIGATING THE GREY

Embracing A Journey in Engineering and Growth

“What do you want to be when you grow older?” they said. “An engineer,” I replied. As far back as I can remember that has always been my answer. Maybe, I have added in a few professions but becoming an engineer has always been a constant. Even now, that I have my degree, my answer has not changed, becoming an engineer is a life-long process. There is so much I can venture into, so much I can learn from; an endless cycle with a different start and end point.

Much like everyone else, it was hard for me to make sense of a world where the colour grey could exist and not just exist but to have a personality of its own; a thriving ambience. That is exactly where I want to be – the lesser known densely populated grey area – constantly growing.

FROM DETOURS TO DISCOVERIES

Finding Resilience and Growth in Every Path Taken

Four years ago, I wanted to become a petroleum engineer and I thought the only way to become one was by getting a bachelor's degree in Petroleum Engineering. My understanding was that the shortest path between two points is the direct length between those two points. Even though I could almost picture myself graduating with a BS in Petroleum Engineering, I had to cut my dream short. I enrolled in a program that cannot be closely or loosely linked to Petroleum Engineering.

Three months into my degree, I had an epiphany — one that I'm grateful for — the colour grey has a thriving ambience. It does not matter if I have to take a longer path, it does not matter if I have to change my path entirely, as long as I'm persistent and resilient in what I do. Nothing else matters. There is quote in a book I once read “sometimes the only way to learn is to live” I think that the only times we do actually learn is when we live through our experiences.
